Physics in detail Física en detalle Physics in detail Physics in detail Physics in detail

Some quantities need to be redefined, as it is convenient for the purposes of this work. These are:
  • Reduced electric charge: \( \qred = \dfrac{q_e}{\epsilon_0} \), with dimensions of \( \dfrac{\mbox{(length)}^3\ \mbox{(mass)}}{\mbox{(time)}^2\ \mbox{(charge)}} \)

  • Reduced electric field: \( \Evec = c\ \epsilon_0\ \Eorig \), with dimensions of \( \dfrac{\mbox{(charge)}}{\mbox{(time)}\ \mbox{(length)}} \)

  • Reduced magnetic field: \( \Mvec = \dfrac{\Morig}{\mu_0} \), with dimensions of \( \dfrac{\mbox{(charge)}}{\mbox{(time)}\ \mbox{(length)}} \)
In special relativity, quantities can be observed from different inertial frames of reference. To change from one frame to another, a Lorentz transformation is used. This transformation can perform a translation, a rotation and/or a change in velocity (boost). Two particular cases of this transformation (a Lorentz boost with rapidity \( \boldsymbol{\beta} \) and gamma factor \( \gamma = (1 - \beta^2)^{-1/2} \) ) are important in this simulation:
  • The time and position coordinates transform as follows:
    • \( c t\prime = \gamma c t - \gamma \boldsymbol{\beta} \cdot \boldsymbol{x} \)
    • \( \boldsymbol{x}\prime = \boldsymbol{x} -\gamma \boldsymbol{\beta} c t + (\gamma - 1) \UnitV_\beta \UnitV_\beta \cdot \boldsymbol{x} \)

  • The electromagnetic field transforms as follows:
    • \( \Evec \rightarrow \Evec\prime = \gamma \left( \Evec - \Mvec \times \boldsymbol{\beta} \right) - (\gamma - 1) \UnitV_\beta \UnitV_\beta \cdot \Evec \)
    • \( \Mvec \rightarrow \Mvec\prime = \gamma \left( \Mvec + \Evec \times \boldsymbol{\beta} \right) - (\gamma - 1) \UnitV_\beta \UnitV_\beta \cdot \Mvec \)
The relativistic equations of motion read:
  • \( \dfrac{m c^2}{\qred} \dfrac{\diff}{\diff t} \left[ \gamma \right] = \Evec \cdot \dfrac{\boldsymbol{u}}{c} \)

  • \( \dfrac{m c^2}{\qred} \dfrac{\diff}{\diff t} \left[ \gamma \dfrac{\boldsymbol{u}}{c} \right] = \Evec - \Mvec \times \dfrac{\boldsymbol{u}}{c} \)
Subject to the initial conditions:
  • \( \boldsymbol{x}(t = 0) = \boldsymbol{x}_0 \)
  • \( \boldsymbol{u}(t = 0) = \boldsymbol{u}_0, \qquad \gamma_0 = \gamma \left( \boldsymbol{u}_0 \right) \)
To solve this equations, we first compute two quantities, the scalar invariant \( \mathsf{I_s} \) and the pseudoscalar invariant \( \mathsf{I_p} \). These have the same value in every inertial frame of reference (up to a sign in the case of \( \mathsf{I_p} \)):
  • \( \mathsf{I_s} = - \Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 \)

  • \( \mathsf{I_p} = - \Evec \cdot \Mvec \)
Depending on the value of \( \mathsf{I_s} \) and \( \mathsf{I_p} \), one of the following cases apply:
  1. If \( \Evec \cdot \Mvec = 0 \) and
    1. if \(-\Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 = 0\), then \(\Evec \perp \Mvec\) and \(\Efield = \Mfield\) for all inertial frames. In this case the electromagnetic field is of null type.

    2. if \(-\Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 < 0\), then there exists a Lorentz boost such that \(\Evec\prime \ne \boldsymbol{0}\) and \(\Mvec\prime = \boldsymbol{0}\). In this case the electromagnetic field is of electric type. The relevant parameters of the transformation are:
      • \( \boldsymbol{\beta} = \dfrac{\Evec \times \Mvec}{ \Efield^2 } \)
      • \( \gamma = \Efield \left( \Efield^2 - \Mfield^2 \right)^{-1/2} \)
      • \( \Evec\prime = \left( \Efield^2 - \Mfield^2 \right)^{1/2} \UnitV_{_\Efield} \)

    3. if \(-\Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 > 0\), then there exists a Lorentz boost such that \(\Evec\prime = \boldsymbol{0}\) and \(\Mvec\prime \ne \boldsymbol{0}\). In this case the electromagnetic field is of magnetic type. The relevant parameters of the transformation are:
      • \( \boldsymbol{\beta} = \dfrac{\Evec \times \Mvec}{ \Mfield^2 } \)
      • \( \gamma = \Mfield \left( \Mfield^2 - \Efield^2 \right)^{-1/2} \)
      • \( \Mvec\prime = \left( \Mfield^2 - \Efield^2 \right)^{1/2} \UnitV_{_\Mfield} \)

  2. If \( \Evec \cdot \Mvec \ne 0 \), then there exists a Lorentz boost such that \(\Evec\prime\) and \(\Mvec\prime\) are either parallel or antiparallel; no Lorentz boost can make either \(\Evec\prime = \boldsymbol{0}\) or \(\Mvec\prime = \boldsymbol{0}\). In this case the electromagnetic field is of collinear type. The relevant parameters of the transformation are:
    • \( \boldsymbol{\beta} = \dfrac{ \Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 - R }{ 2 \left( \left(\Efield\Mfield\right)^2 - \left( \Evec \cdot \Mvec\right )^2 \right)} \Evec \times \Mvec \)
    • \( \gamma = \left( \dfrac{ 2 \left( \left( \Efield \Mfield \right)^2 - \left( \Evec \cdot \Mvec\right )^2 \right) }{ R \left( \Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 - R \right) } \right)^{1/2} \)
    • \( \Evec\prime = \left( \dfrac{ 2 \left( \left( \Efield \Mfield \right)^2 - \left( \Evec \cdot \Mvec\right )^2 \right) }{ R \left( \Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 - R \right) } \right)^{1/2} \Evec + \left( \dfrac{ \Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 - R }{ 2 R \left( \left( \Efield \Mfield \right)^2 - \left( \Evec \cdot \Mvec \right )^2 \right) } \right)^{1/2} \left( \left( \Evec \cdot \Mvec \right) \Mvec - \Mfield^2 \Evec \right) \)
    • \( \Mvec\prime = \left( \dfrac{ 2 \left( \left( \Efield \Mfield \right)^2 - \left( \Evec \cdot \Mvec\right )^2 \right) }{ R \left( \Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 - R \right) } \right)^{1/2} \Mvec + \left( \dfrac{ \Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 - R }{ 2 R \left( \left( \Efield \Mfield \right)^2 - \left( \Evec \cdot \Mvec \right )^2 \right) } \right)^{1/2} \left( \left( \Evec \cdot \Mvec \right) \Evec - \Efield^2 \Mvec \right) \)

    where \( R = \left( \left( -\Efield^2 + \Mfield^2 \right)^2 + 4 \left( \Evec \cdot \Mvec \right)^2 \right)^{1/2} \)

Solution for the Null type field

Rescaling of the fields:
  • \( \Evec \rightarrow \boldsymbol{\Ered} = \dfrac{\qred}{m c^2} \Evec \)

  • \( \Mvec \rightarrow \boldsymbol{\Mred} = \dfrac{\qred}{m c^2} \Mvec \)

  • \( \boldsymbol{u} \rightarrow \boldsymbol{v} = \dfrac{\boldsymbol{u}}{c} \)
Both \( \boldsymbol{\Ered} \) and \( \boldsymbol{\Mred} \) have dimensions of inverse time.

Being the two fields perpendicular and equal in magnitude, two specific unit directions are chosen so that they align with the vector fields \(\boldsymbol{\Ered}\) and \(\boldsymbol{\Mred}\), equating their magnitudes to a common value \(\Fred\):
\(\boldsymbol{\Ered} = \UnitV_1 \Fred, \quad \boldsymbol{\Mred} = \UnitV_2 \Fred \)

and a third unit direction \(\UnitV_3\) completes a right-handed coordinate system: \(\UnitV_1 \times \UnitV_2 = \UnitV_3\).

The solution is given by
  • \( x_1(t) = K_1 + \dfrac{\gamma_0 c}{\Fred} \dfrac{2 \left( 1 - v_{30} \right) - v_{10}^2}{1 - v_{30}} \dfrac{1}{1 - \cos \left( \phi \right)} \)
  • \( x_2(t) = K_2 + \dfrac{\gamma_0 c v_{20}}{\Fred} \dfrac{\left( 2 \left( 1 - v_{30} \right) - v_{10}^2 \right)^{1/2}}{1 - v_{30}} \dfrac{\sin \left( \phi \right)}{1 - \cos \left( \phi \right)} \)
  • \( x_3(t) = K_3 + \dfrac{1 - v_{30}^2 - v_{10}^2}{2 \left( 1 - v_{30} \right) - v_{10}^2} c t - \dfrac{\gamma_0 c}{\Fred} \left( 2 \left( 1 - v_{30} \right) - v_{10}^2 \right)^{1/2} \dfrac{\sin \left( \phi \right) \left[ 1 - 2 \cos \left( \phi \right) \right]}{3 \left[ 1 - \cos \left( \phi \right) \right]^2} \)
with \( K_1 \), \( K_2 \) and \( K_3 \) constants such that the initial conditions are satisfied, and
  • \( \dfrac{1}{1 - \cos \left( \phi \right)} = \dfrac{1}{2} + \dfrac{2T^2}{\left( 1 + r_{_+} + r_{_-} \right)^2} \)
  • \( \dfrac{\sin \left( \phi \right)}{1 - \cos \left( \phi \right)} = \dfrac{2T}{ 1 + r_{_+} + r_{_-} } \)
  • \( \dfrac{\sin \left( \phi \right) \left[ 1 - 2 \cos \left( \phi \right) \right]}{3 \left[ 1 - \cos \left( \phi \right) \right]^2} = \dfrac{T}{1 + r_{_+} + r_{_-}} - \dfrac{4T^3}{3 \left( 1 + r_{_+} + r_{_-} \right)^3} \)
where
  • \( r_{_+} = \left( 1 + 2T \left( 1 + T^2 \right)^{1/2} + 2T^2 \right)^{1/3} \)
  • \( r_{_-} = \left( 1 - 2T \left( 1 + T^2 \right)^{1/2} + 2T^2 \right)^{1/3} \)
\( T \) is an adimensional rescaling of time: \( T = \left( 2 \left( 1 - v_{30} \right) - v_{10}^2 \right)^{-3/2} \left[ v_{10} \left( 3 \left( 1 - v_{30} \right) - v_{10}^2 \right) + \dfrac{3 \Fred}{\gamma_0} \left( 1 - v_{30} \right)^2 t \right] \)

And the characteristic time of the system \(\mathcal{T}_c\) is the value of \( t \) for which \( T = 1 \). In general, \(\mathcal{T}_c(N)\) is a function of \( N \), the number of pseudo-cycles of motion:

\( \mathcal{T}_c(N) = \dfrac{ \gamma_0 }{ 3 \Fred \left( 1 - v_{30} \right)^2 } \left[ -v_{10} \left( 3 \left( 1 - v_{30} \right) - v_{10}^2 \right) + \left( 2 \left( 1 - v_{30} \right) - v_{10}^2 \right)^{3/2} N \right] \)

Solution for the Electric type field

Rescaling of the initial velocity and the electric field:
  • \( \boldsymbol{u}_0 \longrightarrow \uzscaled = \gamma_0 \dfrac{\boldsymbol{u}_0}{c} \)

  • \( \Evec \longrightarrow \Focvec = \dfrac{\qred}{m c^2} \Evec \)
\( \Focvec \) has dimensions of inverse time; it is called the focal vector because the solution of the equations of motion corresponds to an hyperbola with focal length \( 2^{1/2}\ c\ / \lvert \Focvec \rvert \). The solution is given by
\( \boldsymbol{x}(t) = \boldsymbol{x}_0 + \dfrac{c}{|\Focvec|} \left[ \left( \left[ 1 + \left( \uzscaled + \Focvec t \right)^2 \right]^{1/2} - \left[ 1 + \uzscaled^2 \right]^{1/2} \right) \UnitV_{_{\Focvec}} + \ln \left( \dfrac{ |\Focvec| \left[ 1 + \left( \uzscaled + \Focvec t \right)^2 \right]^{1/2} + \Focvec \cdot \left( \uzscaled + \Focvec t \right) }{ |\Focvec| \left[ 1 + \uzscaled^2 \right]^{1/2} + \Focvec \cdot \uzscaled } \right) \left( \uzscaled - \UnitV_{_{\Focvec}} \UnitV_{_{\Focvec}} \cdot \uzscaled \right) \right] \)
The characteristic time of the system is given by
\( \mathcal{T}_\subE = \dfrac{1}{\lvert \Focvec \rvert} = \dfrac{m c^2}{\qred \Efield} \)

\( \Omvec \) is called the angular frequency vector and it has dimensions of inverse time. The solution is given by

Solution for the Collinear type field

Rescaling of the fields: the same as in the null-type case.

A specific unit direction is chosen explicitly to describe the fields, writing:
\( \boldsymbol{\Ered} = \UnitV_\parallel \Ered, \quad \boldsymbol{\Mred} = \UnitV_\parallel \Mred \)

where \(\Ered\) and \(\Mred\) are allowed to be signed, in order to consider both cases in which the fields are either parallel or antiparallel. The particular direction \(\UnitV_\parallel\) is chosen as the \(\UnitV_1\) axis, and the unit directions \(\UnitV_2\) and \(\UnitV_3\) form a right-handed coordinate system (\(\UnitV_1 \times \UnitV_2 = \UnitV_3\)). The solution is given by
  • \( x_1(t) = x_{10} + \dfrac{c}{\Ered} \left[ -\gamma_0 + \left( \gamma_0^2 (1 - v_{10}^2) + \left( \gamma_0 v_{10} + \Ered t \right)^2 \right)^{1/2} \right] \)
  • \( x_2(t) = x_{20} + \dfrac{\gamma_0 c}{\Mred} \left[ v_{30} \left( \cos \left( \dfrac{\Mred}{\Ered} \phi \right) - 1 \right) + v_{20} \sin \left( \dfrac{\Mred}{\Ered} \phi \right) \right] \)
  • \( x_3(t) = x_{30} + \dfrac{\gamma_0 c}{\Mred} \left[ -v_{20} \left( \cos \left( \dfrac{\Mred}{\Ered} \phi \right) - 1 \right) + v_{30} \sin \left( \dfrac{\Mred}{\Ered} \phi \right) \right] \)

where
\( \phi = \ln \left( \dfrac{ \gamma_0 v_{10} + \Ered t + \left( \gamma_0^2 (1 - v_{10}^2) + \left( \gamma_0 v_{10} + \Ered t \right)^2 \right)^{1/2} }{ \gamma_0 \left( 1 + v_{10}\right) } \right) \)

The characteristic time \(\mathcal{T}_c(N)\) is a function of \(N\), the number of pseudo-cycles of motion:

\( \mathcal{T}_c(N) = \dfrac{\gamma_0}{\Ered} \left[ v_{10} \left( \cosh \left( \dfrac{\Ered}{\Mred} N \tau \right) - 1 \right) + \sinh \left( \dfrac{\Ered}{\Mred} N \tau \right) \right] \)

Additional transformations needed to solve the equations of motion

In the null and collinear cases, an additional coordinate transformation (a rotation) is needed in order to achieve the solutions to the equations of motion. In general, the rotation operator \( R \) which transforms the unit vector \( \UnitV_s \) into the unit vector \( \UnitV_r \) can be written as
\( R = \mathbb{1} + \left( \UnitV_s \times \UnitV_r \right) \times + \dfrac{1}{1 + \UnitV_s \cdot \UnitV_r} \left[ \left( \UnitV_s \times \UnitV_r \right) \times \right]^2 \)

and the inverse transformation \( R^{-1} \) is achieved by interchanging \( \UnitV_s \) and \( \UnitV_r \).

The only case in which the expression above is not valid is when \( \UnitV_s \cdot \UnitV_r = -1 \), i.e., when \( \UnitV_s \) and \( \UnitV_r \) are antiparallel. In this case, the transformation operator is an inversion, \( R = -\mathbb{1} \).

In the particular cases needed for this work, the following expressions are used:
  • When the field is of null type, a first rotation is performed in order to align \( (u_1, u_2, u_3) \), the unit direction of \( \Evec \times \Mvec \), with the \( x_3 \) axis, in which case the rotation matrix \( R_3 \) reads
    \( R_3 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 - \dfrac{u_1^2}{1 + u_3} & -\dfrac{u_1 u_2}{1 + u_3} & -u_1 \\ -\dfrac{u_1 u_2}{1 + u_3} & 1 - \dfrac{u_2^2}{1 + u_3} & -u_2 \\ u_1 & u_2 & 1 - \dfrac{u_1^2 + u_2^2}{1 + u_3} \end{pmatrix} \)

    and a second rotation is performed to align \( (u_1, u_2, u_3) \), the unit direction of the just-transformed \( \Evec \), with the \( x_1 \) axis, in which case the rotation matrix \( R_1 \) reads
    \( R_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 - \dfrac{u_2^2 + u_3^2}{1 + u_1} & u_2 & u_3 \\ -u_2 & 1 - \dfrac{u_2^2}{1 + u_1} & -\dfrac{u_2 u_3}{1 + u_1} \\ -u_3 & -\dfrac{u_2 u_3}{1 + u_1} & 1 - \dfrac{u_3^2}{1 + u_1} \end{pmatrix} \)


  • When the field is of collinear type, a single rotation is performed to align the \( \Evec \) field with the \( x_1 \) axis; the transformation matrix is \( R_1 \), with the same expression as the one shown above.
